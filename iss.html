View From the Station

This is a simulation of the live view from the International Space Station (within 5 seconds). Position and velocity are supplied by live telemetry. The Earth, Stars, Moon, Sun, and planets are placed using real orbital mechanics.

The ISS orbits at approximately 408 km altitude, completing one orbit every 90 minutes. Day and night cycle every 45 minutes.

Drag to look around. Select a camera lens to zoom. The Eye view is set to a 25mm equivalent lens.
